# Caveats

Known pitfalls, past mistakes, and things to watch out for. Add an entry whenever an error costs time so it is not repeated.

## Formalization pitfalls

- **No trace-class/Schatten-class theory in Mathlib (as of the Mathlib revision pinned in `lakefile.toml`).** `LinearMap.trace` requires finite-dimensionality. This blocks a direct extension of `QuantumTheory.DensityOperator` (`LeanCondensedMatter/QuantumTheory/DensityOperator.lean`) to the countably-infinite-dimensional lattice setting chosen in `notes/model-and-assumptions.md` for the Linked Cluster Theorem target ŌĆö `Z = tr e^{-╬▓H}` needs a trace on an infinite-dimensional space. Until such machinery exists (in Mathlib or built here), density-operator work stays scoped to finite-dimensional `H`.

- **Partition-lattice M├Čbius closed-form formula `╬╝(ŽĆ,Žā) = (-1)^(|ŽĆ|-|Žā|) ŌłÅ(n_B-1)!` is genuinely hard to formalize ŌĆö three routes tried, all blocked on missing Mathlib lemmas.** Context: `LeanCondensedMatter/Combinatorics/PartitionLattice.lean` has a `LocallyFiniteOrder (Finpartition s)` instance (done, builds cleanly), which lets Mathlib's `IncidenceAlgebra.mu`/`moebius_inversion_top`/`bot` apply to the partition lattice. But the *closed-form value* of `mu` on the partition lattice is not in Mathlib and needs its own proof. Three approaches were tried in one session, each hitting a different missing-lemma wall:
  1. **Order-isomorphism invariance of `mu` across different poset types**, i.e. a general theorem `[a,b] Ōēā [a',b']` (order-iso) `Ō¤╣ ╬╝(a,b) = ╬╝(a',b')`, to be combined with the interval-structure isomorphism `[ŽĆ,Žā] Ōēģ ŌłÅ_{BŌłłŽā} Finpartition(ŽĆ_B)` (mirroring the standard textbook proof, e.g. Stanley EC1). Attempted directly by strong induction on `(Finset.Icc a b).card`, transporting the recursive sum via the bijection. The bookkeeping (mapping `Set.Ico`/`Finset.Ico` membership back and forth across the two posets, restricting the bijection to sub-intervals for the inductive step) got tangled and was abandoned mid-proof (not committed).
  2. **Algebraic route via inverse-uniqueness in the incidence algebra**, mirroring how Mathlib itself proves `IncidenceAlgebra.mu_prod_mu` (product-poset multiplicativity) ŌĆö by showing a candidate function is `╬Č`'s inverse (`f * zeta = 1`) rather than re-deriving uniqueness by hand. This avoids induction for the *product* case, but still needs an order-iso transport step to connect `[ŽĆ,Žā]` (living in `Finpartition S`) to the literal product poset `ŌłÅ_B Finpartition(ŽĆ_B)` (a different type) ŌĆö i.e. it doesn't eliminate route 1's core difficulty, only reduces how much of the argument needs hand-rolled induction.
  3. **Direct strong induction on `|ŽĆ|` (block count) entirely within `Finpartition S`**, using Mathlib's `mu_eq_neg_sum_Ioc_of_ne` (right-recursion: `╬╝(ŽĆ,ŌŖż) = -╬Ż_{ŽĆ<ŽüŌēżŌŖż} ╬╝(Žü,ŌŖż)`) ŌĆö avoids crossing poset types at all, since strict refinement always strictly decreases block count. This reduces the goal to a *Stirling-number identity*: `╬Ż_{j=1}^{k} S(k,j)(-1)^{j-1}(j-1)! = 0` (`S` = Stirling numbers of the second kind, `Nat.stirlingSecond` in Mathlib), plus a bijective correspondence between "coarsenings of a `k`-block partition into `j` blocks" and `Nat.stirlingSecond k j`. **Neither exists in Mathlib** (checked `Mathlib/Combinatorics/Enumerative/Stirling.lean` ŌĆö only basic recurrences, no cross-Stirling-kind alternating-sum identity).
  - **Conclusion:** all three routes bottom out in a genuinely-missing Mathlib lemma, not just tedious bookkeeping. This is realistically a multi-session undertaking on its own. If retried, route 3 (Stirling-number identity) is probably the most self-contained next attempt, since it stays within one poset type and reduces to a single well-defined numeric identity rather than type-crossing isomorphism transport.

- **The *abstract* cumulant formula (`c(S) = ╬Ż_ŽĆ mu(ŽĆ,ŌŖż) ŌłÅ m(B)`, via M├Čbius inversion) does not need the closed-form M├Čbius formula above ŌĆö but still needs its own nontrivial bijection.** Realization: M├Čbius *inversion* itself (`IncidenceAlgebra.moebius_inversion_top`/`bot`, already usable via the `LocallyFiniteOrder (Finpartition s)` instance) only needs the *abstract* `mu`, not its closed-form value ŌĆö so the cumulant formula can in principle be derived now, without waiting on the closed-form-M├Čbius problem above. What it does need is the decomposition `G(Žā) = ╬Ż_{ŽĆŌēżŽā} F(ŽĆ)` (`G(Žā) := ŌłÅ_{BŌłłŽā.parts} m(B)`, `F(Žā) := ŌłÅ_{BŌłłŽā.parts} c(B)`, given the moment-cumulant hypothesis `m(T) = ╬Ż_{ŽĆ:Finpartition T} ŌłÅ_{BŌłłŽĆ.parts} c(B)`), which itself needs the bijection `{ŽĆ // ŽĆ Ōēż Žā} Ōēā ╬Ā B Ōłł Žā.parts, Finpartition B`.
  - **Progress made:** `Finpartition.bind_le` (`P.bind Q Ōēż P`) and `Finpartition.bind_restrict_eq_of_le` (`ŽĆ Ōēż Žā ŌåÆ Žā.bind (fun B hB => ŽĆ.restrict (Žā.le hB)) = ŽĆ`, the "gluing recovers the original" direction) are proved in `LeanCondensedMatter/Combinatorics/PartitionLattice.lean`.
  - **Still needed:** (a) the *other* round-trip direction (`(Žā.bind Q).restrict (Žā.le hB) = Q B hB`), to get a genuine bijection rather than a one-directional map; (b) converting the "product of sums = sum over choice-functions of products" step (`ŌłÅ_{BŌłłŽā.parts} m(B) = ╬Ż_{(Žü_B)_B} ŌłÅ_B ŌłÅ_{CŌłłŽü_B.parts} c(C)`) into Mathlib's `Finset.prod_sum`/`Finset.pi` framework, which expects each per-index choice set to be a `Finset` inside a common ambient ("`s.pi t`") type ŌĆö `Finpartition B` is a `Fintype`, not literally elements of a `Finset` in a shared ambient type, so connecting the two frameworks is itself nontrivial work, not yet attempted.

- **`cfc f T v = f(c) ŌĆó v` for an eigenvector `v` (`T v = c ŌĆó v`) is not in Mathlib and requires a genuine limit argument to prove ŌĆö checkpointed partway.** Context: `LeanCondensedMatter/Analysis/CFC.lean`, part of Track C (`notes/roadmaps/operator-algebra.md`) groundwork. Mathlib's continuous functional calculus `cfc` (confirmed usable on `H ŌåÆL[Ōäé] H` via its `CStarAlgebra` instance) has no lemma connecting it to eigenvectors. Two routes were surveyed: (1) polynomial approximation (StoneŌĆōWeierstrass density + `isometry_cfcHom` to pass to the limit), (2) transfer to `Matrix.IsHermitian` via a star-algebra isomorphism built from an eigenbasis ŌĆö route (2) needs a `StarAlgEquiv (H ŌåÆL[Ōäé] H) (Matrix (Fin n) (Fin n) Ōäé)` that does not exist in Mathlib and would be substantial new infrastructure, so route (1) was chosen.
  - **Done:** `Polynomial.aeval_apply_eigenvector` ŌĆö the polynomial-functional-calculus case (`(Polynomial.aeval T q) v = (q.eval c : Ōäé) ŌĆó v` for `q : ŌäØ[X]`), proved by induction on `q` (`LeanCondensedMatter/Analysis/CFC.lean`).
  - **Still needed:** the limit step from polynomials to general continuous `f`, roughly: (a) `c Ōłł spectrum ŌäØ T` (from `v` being a nonzero eigenvector ŌĆö `IsSelfAdjoint.hasEigenvalue_iff_mem_spectrum` in `Mathlib/Analysis/InnerProductSpace/Spectrum.lean` plus a `HasEigenvalue`-from-eigenvector lemma), (b) compactness of `spectrum ŌäØ T` (standard for bounded operators, should already be in Mathlib), (c) a polynomial sequence `qŌéÖ` converging uniformly to `f` on `spectrum ŌäØ T` via `polynomialFunctions.topologicalClosure` (`Mathlib/Topology/ContinuousMap/StoneWeierstrass.lean`) plus `mem_closure_iff_seq_limit`, (d) `isometry_cfcHom` (`Mathlib/Analysis/CStarAlgebra/ContinuousFunctionalCalculus/Isometric.lean`) to transfer uniform convergence of `qŌéÖ` to operator-norm convergence of `cfc qŌéÖ T ŌåÆ cfc f T`, (e) uniqueness of limits combining (d) with `cfc qŌéÖ T v = (qŌéÖ.eval c : Ōäé) ŌĆó v ŌåÆ (f c : Ōäé) ŌĆó v` (continuity of evaluation at `c`). None of steps (a)ŌĆō(e) individually looked blocked when surveyed, but assembling them is a genuine multi-step proof, not a lemma application ŌĆö checkpointed here rather than pushed through in one session.
