## Density operators and the Born rule (finite-dimensional)

Status: `stated`.

Density-operator postulate (`QuantumTheory.DensityOperator`, positive trace-1 operator) and general (POVM) measurement postulate (`QuantumTheory.POVM`, `QuantumTheory.prob`), with the Born rule's probabilities proved to sum to `1` (`sum_prob_eq_one`). Purification (`QuantumTheory.pure`) and purity (`QuantumTheory.purity`) defined, with `purity_pure : purity (pure Žł) = 1` proved. See `LeanCondensedMatter/QuantumTheory/DensityOperator.lean`. **Scoped to finite-dimensional `H`** ŌĆö see the trace-class caveat in `notes/caveats.md`.

**In progress: infinite-dimensional density operators.** Now that Track C's `ContinuousLinearMap.trace` (with linearity/cyclicity) is done, extending `DensityOperator` beyond finite dimensions is underway in `LeanCondensedMatter/QuantumTheory/DensityOperatorTraceClass.lean` (namespace `QuantumTheory.TraceClass`, additive to the finite-dimensional file above ŌĆö nothing there is touched). `QuantumTheory.TraceClass.DensityOperator` (positive + compact + trace-class + trace `1`) is defined, and `QuantumTheory.TraceClass.pure : State H ŌåÆ DensityOperator H` (the rank-one projector `|ŽłŌ¤®Ō¤©Žł|`) is **fully proved** for arbitrary (possibly infinite-dimensional) `H`:
- `isCompactOperator_rankOne` ŌĆö any rank-one operator `|xŌ¤®Ō¤©y|` is compact, via factoring through the locally-compact `Ōäé`.
- `eigenspace_rankOne_eq_bot`/`eigenspace_rankOne_one` ŌĆö for unit `Žł`, `|ŽłŌ¤®Ō¤©Žł|`'s only nonzero eigenvalue is `1`, with eigenspace exactly `span {Žł}` (computed directly from the rank-one operator's formula, rather than via a general "finite rank" argument).
- `uniqueEigenvectorIndexRankOne` ŌĆö consequently `EigenvectorIndex |ŽłŌ¤®Ō¤©Žł|` has a *unique* element (every other eigenvalue's `Fin`-indexed fiber is empty), giving `rankOne_isTraceClass`/`rankOne_trace_eq_one` (trace-class, with trace exactly `1`, matching the physical `Tr[|ŽłŌ¤®Ō¤©Žł|] = 1`) essentially for free.

Recurring Lean pitfall in this proof (not a math issue): `haveI hu := someDef args` makes `hu` an *opaque* local hypothesis, **not** definitionally equal to `someDef args` (unlike `let`) ŌĆö so a later `show` restating the goal in terms of `someDef args` directly can fail to unify with a goal stated in terms of `hu`. Fix: either avoid the abbreviation and repeat `someDef args` at each use site, or use `let`/`set` (which preserve defeq) instead of `have`/`haveI` when you need to unfold back to the original term later.

**`POVM`/`prob`/`sum_prob_eq_one` are now ported (infinite-dimensional), in `QuantumTheory.TraceClass`.** The originally-anticipated blocker ŌĆö `E_m Ōłś Žü` need not be self-adjoint, so `ContinuousLinearMap.trace` doesn't directly apply ŌĆö turned out to have a simpler resolution than the HilbertŌĆōSchmidt inner product route developed for it (`notes/roadmaps/operator-algebra.md`): `prob` is defined directly via `Žü`'s *own* eigendecomposition (`ContinuousLinearMap.EigenvectorIndex`/`eigenvectorFamily`, from `Analysis/CompactSelfAdjoint.lean`) rather than via a general basis-independent trace of `E_m Ōłś Žü`:
- `QuantumTheory.TraceClass.POVM` ŌĆö a finite family of positive bounded operators summing to the identity. Unlike the finite-dimensional `QuantumTheory.POVM`, the individual `E m` need *not* be compact or trace-class (e.g. a single-outcome POVM forces `E () = 1`, never compact in infinite dimensions) ŌĆö the definition only uses `Žü`'s trace-class-ness, not `E_m`'s.
- `QuantumTheory.TraceClass.prob P Žü m := (╬ŻßĄó ╬╗ßĄó Ō¤¬eßĄó, E_m eßĄóŌ¤½).re`, summing over `Žü`'s eigenvector family `e` with eigenvalues `╬╗`. Well-defined (`summable_prob_term`) by comparing `|╬╗ßĄó Ō¤¬eßĄó,E_m eßĄóŌ¤®| Ōēż |╬╗ßĄó|┬ĘŌĆ¢E_mŌĆ¢` (each `eßĄó` a unit vector, `IsTraceClass Žü.op` giving `╬Ż|╬╗ßĄó|` summable) against the trace-class hypothesis.
- `QuantumTheory.TraceClass.sum_prob_eq_one` ŌĆö proved by swapping the finite sum over outcomes `M` with the (absolutely convergent) sum over `Žü`'s eigenvectors (`Summable.tsum_finsetSum`), using `P.sum_eq_id` to collapse `╬ŻŌéś E_m eßĄó` back to `eßĄó` (via `Ō¤¬eßĄó,eßĄóŌ¤® = 1`, unit vectors), and `Žü.trace_eq_one` to evaluate the resulting eigenvalue sum.

This sidesteps needing `E_m` itself to be HilbertŌĆōSchmidt (which the `innerHS`-based route would have required), since only `Žü`'s eigenbasis ŌĆö not a general Hilbert basis ŌĆö is ever used; the HilbertŌĆōSchmidt inner product infrastructure (steps 1ŌĆō4, complete) remains available for other purposes but wasn't needed for the Born rule after all.

**Still needed: `purity` (`Tr[Žü┬▓]`) ŌĆö deliberately deferred, not just unstarted.** `Žü Ōłś Žü` is compact and self-adjoint for free, but proving it's *trace-class* needs a new spectral-theory lemma not yet in `Analysis/CompactSelfAdjoint.lean`: for self-adjoint `T`, `Module.End.eigenspace (T Ōłś T) ╬Į = Module.End.eigenspace T ŌłÜ╬Į` for `╬Į > 0` (simpler than the fully general statement since `Žü` is positive, so has no negative eigenvalues, ruling out the `eigenspace T (-ŌłÜ╬Į)` contribution that would otherwise also merge in). This is comparable in scope to the existing eigenvector-family machinery in that file, not a quick corollary ŌĆö considered and consciously deferred (2026-07-14) in favor of other targets rather than attempted piecemeal.

**Von Neumann entropy is now ported (infinite-dimensional), in `LeanCondensedMatter/QuantumTheory/EntropyTraceClass.lean` (namespace `QuantumTheory.TraceClass`, additive to `QuantumTheory/Entropy.lean`).** `QuantumTheory.TraceClass.vonNeumannEntropy` computes `-╬ŻßĄó ╬╗ßĄó ln ╬╗ßĄó` from `Žü`'s eigenvalues via `ContinuousLinearMap.EigenvectorIndex`, just as `prob` above does.

**A genuine mathematical wrinkle, not a Lean technicality:** unlike the finite-dimensional `ŌäØ`-valued `vonNeumannEntropy` (a finite sum, automatically finite), the infinite-dimensional entropy sum `╬ŻßĄó (-╬╗ßĄó ln ╬╗ßĄó)` ŌĆö every term nonnegative ŌĆö **can genuinely diverge** even though `╬ŻßĄó ╬╗ßĄó` converges (`Žü` is trace-class): e.g. `╬╗ßĄó = c/(i log┬▓ i)` is summable, but `-╬╗ßĄó ln ╬╗ßĄó ~ c/(i log i)` is not. A trace-class density operator really can have infinite von Neumann entropy ŌĆö this is standard in the physics literature, not a formalization artifact. So `vonNeumannEntropy` is **`ENNReal`-valued** (`[0, Ōł×]`) rather than `ŌäØ`-valued: the `tsum` is always well-defined in that codomain (divergence shows up honestly as `ŌŖż`), avoiding the silent-junk-value-`0` problem a real-valued `tsum` would have for a non-summable sequence. `eigenvalue_nonneg` (needed to justify treating each `╬╗ßĄó` as a probability) is proved via Mathlib's `eigenvalue_nonneg_of_nonneg`, mirroring `ContinuousLinearMap.trace_nonneg`'s proof.

**`gibbsState` is now ported (infinite-dimensional), in `LeanCondensedMatter/QuantumTheory/GibbsStateTraceClass.lean` (namespace `QuantumTheory.TraceClass`, additive to `QuantumTheory/Entropy.lean`).** Rather than deriving a discrete-spectrum/compact-resolvent condition on `Hop` from more primitive assumptions, `gibbsState` takes compactness and trace-class-ness of the (unnormalized) Gibbs operator `e^{-╬▓H}}`, plus non-vanishing of its trace `Z(╬▓)`, as *explicit hypotheses* ŌĆö matching this project's established style for `DensityOperator`/`POVM` (unnormalized Gibbs operator `e^{-╬▓H}`):
- `gibbsOp Hop ╬▓ := cfc (fun x => Real.exp (-╬▓ * x)) Hop.1` ŌĆö the unnormalized Gibbs operator, via Mathlib's continuous functional calculus `cfc` (no finite-dimensionality needed, unlike the finite-dimensional `gibbsState`'s explicit eigenbasis sum).
- `gibbsOp_isPositive` ŌĆö via `cfc_nonneg` (the CŌŗå-algebra order) bridged to `ContinuousLinearMap.IsPositive` via Mathlib's `nonneg_iff_isPositive`.
- `gibbsState Hop ╬▓ hcompact htc hZ := (trace htc)Ōü╗┬╣ ŌĆó gibbsOp Hop ╬▓` ŌĆö the normalized state, with `pos`/`compact`/`traceClass`/`trace_eq_one` assembled from `gibbsOp_isPositive`, `IsCompactOperator.smul`, the newly-added `ContinuousLinearMap.isTraceClass_smul` (`Analysis/CompactSelfAdjoint.lean`, Track C), and `trace_smul` respectively. `real_smul_eq_complex_smul` bridges the `ŌäØ`-scalar multiple used for normalization (matching `trace_smul`'s convention) with the `Ōäé`-scalar version `IsPositive.smul_of_nonneg` needs.

**`energyExpValue` is now ported (infinite-dimensional), in `LeanCondensedMatter/QuantumTheory/EnergyExpValueTraceClass.lean` (namespace `QuantumTheory.TraceClass`, additive to `QuantumTheory/Entropy.lean`).** The naive analogue of the finite-dimensional `(LinearMap.trace Ōäé H (Žü.1 ŌłśL Hop.1)).re` would route through `ContinuousLinearMap.trace (Žü.op ŌłśL Hop.1)`, but that trace is only meaningful for *compact self-adjoint* operators, and `Žü.op ŌłśL Hop.1` need not be self-adjoint (a composition of two self-adjoint operators is self-adjoint only when they commute) ŌĆö exactly the same obstacle already hit and worked around for the Born-rule `prob` above. So, mirroring `prob`, `QuantumTheory.TraceClass.energyExpValue Žü Hop := (Ōłæ' a : EigenvectorIndex Žü.op, (a.1.1 : Ōäé) * Ō¤¬eßĄó, Hop.1 eßĄóŌ¤½).re` is computed directly from `Žü`'s own eigendecomposition (`ContinuousLinearMap.eigenvectorFamily`), needing no trace-class/compactness assumption on `Hop` beyond it being a general `Observable H`. This is the same quantity as the finite-dimensional `Tr[Žü─ż] = ╬ŻŌéś p_m Ō¤©e_m|─ż|e_mŌ¤®` (`QuantumTheory.energyExpValue_eq_sum`, specialized to `Žü`'s eigenbasis alone rather than a double sum over both `Žü`'s and `Hop`'s eigenbases). Summability (`summable_energyExpValue_term`) is proved by the same `ŌĆ¢Hop.1ŌĆ¢`-norm-comparison argument as `summable_prob_term`.

**The GibbsŌĆōKlein / Helmholtz free-energy inequality is now ported (infinite-dimensional), in `LeanCondensedMatter/QuantumTheory/HelmholtzFreeEnergyTraceClass.lean` (namespace `QuantumTheory.TraceClass` for the Hilbert-space-specific lemmas; a handful of general real-analysis/infinite-sum lemmas are stated at the top level).** `QuantumTheory.TraceClass.helmholtzFreeEnergy_ge_and_entropy_ne_top` proves, for a density operator `Žü`, Hamiltonian `Hop`, `╬▓ > 0`, and explicit compactness/trace-class/nonzero-trace hypotheses on the (unnormalized) Gibbs operator `gibbsOp Hop ╬▓` (matching `gibbsState`'s own hypotheses), both `-(1/╬▓)┬Ęln Z(╬▓) Ōēż energyExpValue Žü Hop - (1/╬▓)┬Ę(vonNeumannEntropy Žü).toReal` and, as a byproduct, `vonNeumannEntropy Žü ŌēĀ ŌŖż` ŌĆö i.e. under these hypotheses the entropy sum is provably finite, so the previously-open `ENNReal`-formalization ambiguity resolves itself: a plain `ŌäØ`-valued inequality suffices once finiteness is established as part of the same proof, with no need for the `EReal`-statement route considered earlier.

The Mathlib route originally hoped for (a spectral measure at a vector, with Jensen's inequality) does not exist at the pinned Mathlib revision (no `spectralMeasure` declaration, no Riesz-representation path from `cfcHom` to a measure) ŌĆö see `notes/roadmaps/operator-algebra.md`'s "PeierlsŌĆōBogoliubov spectral inequality" section for the tangent-line-trick alternative actually used (`Analysis/PeierlsBogoliubov.lean`). The free-energy proof itself works entirely in `Žü`'s own eigenbasis (`EigenvectorIndex`/`eigenvectorFamily`), combining:
- `exp_neg_beta_energy_le_gibbs_diagonal` ŌĆö PeierlsŌĆōBogoliubov, wrapped into diagonal-matrix-element form against a unit eigenvector.
- `neg_log_le_of_exp_le` and `gibbs_scalar_ineq`/`negMulLog_le_of_neg_log_le` ŌĆö the elementary real-analysis inequalities (`x ln(x/y) Ōēź x - y` and its log/exp consequence) that turn the PeierlsŌĆōBogoliubov bound into a termwise entropy bound.
- `ContinuousLinearMap.sum_inner_apply_le_trace` (new general lemma, `Analysis/CompactSelfAdjoint.lean`) ŌĆö bounds the sum of diagonal matrix elements of a positive trace-class operator against an *incomplete* orthonormal family (here, `Žü`'s own eigenvectors, which need not span `H`) by its trace; proved by extending the family to a full Hilbert basis (`Orthonormal.exists_hilbertBasis_extension`) and comparing against the full-basis trace sum via `hasSum_le_inj`.
- `tsum_div_le_one`, `summable_and_tsum_le_of_nonneg_of_le`, `trace_gibbsOp_pos`, `vonNeumannEntropy_ne_top_and_toReal_eq_tsum`, `summable_eigenvalue_mul_energy_and_tsum`, `eigenvalue_le_one` ŌĆö supporting lemmas factored out so the main theorem's body reads as the physical argument (pointwise bound ŌåÆ summability ŌåÆ sum and rearrange) rather than interleaved bookkeeping.

## Canonical distribution as the Helmholtz free-energy-minimizing state

Status: `stated`.

Goal: formalize that the canonical/Gibbs state `Žü' = e^{-╬▓H}/Z(╬▓)`, `Z(╬▓) = Tr[e^{-╬▓H}]`, is the (unconstrained) minimizer of the Helmholtz free energy `F[Žü] = Tr[Žü─ż] - (1/╬▓)┬ĘvonNeumannEntropy Žü` over all density operators `Žü` ŌĆö *not* "the entropy-maximizing state at fixed energy" (an earlier, less precise phrasing of this target; the free-energy formulation is the one actually derived from GibbsŌĆōKlein, with no separate energy constraint needed). Needed for the finite-temperature theory: this is what identifies `Z(╬▓) = Tr[e^{-╬▓H}]` (used throughout the Linked Cluster Theorem target) as *the* physically realized state, not just a convenient definition.

`QuantumTheory.helmholtzFreeEnergy_ge` ŌĆö **proved** (`LeanCondensedMatter/QuantumTheory/Entropy.lean`): for any density operator `Žü`, Hamiltonian `Hop : Observable H`, and `╬▓ > 0`,
`-(1/╬▓)┬Ęln Z(╬▓) Ōēż energyExpValue Žü Hop - (1/╬▓)┬ĘvonNeumannEntropy Žü`, i.e. `F[Žü] Ōēź -(1/╬▓)┬Ęln Z(╬▓)`. The proof avoids the originally-anticipated blocker (relating `gibbsState`'s spectral data to Mathlib's canonical sorted eigenbasis): it runs the GibbsŌĆōKlein argument directly against `Hop`'s own eigenbasis, using Boltzmann weights `w_k = e^{-╬▓EŌé¢}/Z(╬▓)` as plain functions rather than routing through `gibbsState`/`relEntropy` at all, sidestepping the correspondence problem entirely. `energyExpValue` (`Tr[Žü─ż]`) and its cross-eigenbasis double-sum expansion (`energyExpValue_eq_sum`) were added to support this.

`QuantumTheory.diagOp_eigenvalues_map_eq` ŌĆö **proved** (`LeanCondensedMatter/QuantumTheory/Entropy.lean`): a general lemma showing that for a self-adjoint operator presented diagonally as `Ōłæ i, w i ŌĆó |bßĄóŌ¤®Ō¤©bßĄó|` in a known orthonormal basis `b`, the multiset of weights `w` equals the multiset of the operator's own Mathlib-sorted spectral eigenvalues ŌĆö proved via the characteristic polynomial (basis-independent, `LinearMap.charpoly_toMatrix`) rather than by exhibiting an explicit reindexing permutation. This closes the originally-anticipated blocker directly (rather than avoiding it as `helmholtzFreeEnergy_ge` did).

`QuantumTheory.vonNeumannEntropy_gibbsState` ŌĆö **proved**: `vonNeumannEntropy hn (gibbsState hn Hop ╬▓) = ╬▓ ┬Ę energyExpValue (gibbsState hn Hop ╬▓) Hop + ln Z(╬▓)`, i.e. `gibbsState`'s own Helmholtz free energy is exactly `-(1/╬▓)┬Ęln Z(╬▓)` ŌĆö the lower bound of `helmholtzFreeEnergy_ge` is attained by `gibbsState` itself. Uses `diagOp_eigenvalues_map_eq` to identify `gibbsState`'s own spectral data with its Boltzmann weights without an explicit permutation.

**Remaining to close this target fully:** the equality-iff-`Žü = gibbsState` direction (uniqueness of the minimizer) is not yet formalized ŌĆö only that the bound is achieved (by `gibbsState`) and always valid (`helmholtzFreeEnergy_ge`) are proved.
